I think this album is a big step forward for this band. I appreciate the perspective Augusta Koch has on the world, and while it seems like sometimes she overly relies on repetition, I think there's some really good tunes here and good ideas that I don't see explored anywhere else. I think the journey of Gladie has been one of self-exploration and self-criticism. This is the kind of album that I wish was more broadly appealing, because I'd like to send it to some people I know so that they can hear what it has to say. While it is a little on the nose - *I wanted you so badly to see / the lengths I'd go to get healthy* - I think you just have to shut your brain off and smile and hope that the person behind the making of this song is getting better. I think they are.
